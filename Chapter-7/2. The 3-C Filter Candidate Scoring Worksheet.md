**Scoring guidelines:**

- **Complexity:** How predictable is the path to success? 5 = you’ve done it many times, the variables are known, and the outcome can be defined in two sentences. 3 = some novel elements but manageable. 1 = highly novel, multi-jurisdictional, politically charged.
- **Client Openness:** 5 = client has already asked about alternative fee arrangements or has experimented with outcome-based contracts with their own vendors. 3 = client trusts you but hasn’t signaled any particular interest in fee innovation. 1 = client has explicitly demanded hourly billing in the past and would likely escalate any change.
- **Containable Scope:** 5 = single deliverable, clear endpoint, time-bound (under 3 months). 3 = defined project but with some flexibility. 1 = ongoing advisory, retainer-style, no natural finish line.

**Selection rules:**  
- Highest total score wins.  
- If tied, prioritize Client Openness. Trust can compensate for moderate complexity, but no amount of simplicity saves you from a hostile client.  
- If no candidate scores above 10 total, you may need to wait for a better opportunity or invest in client education first (Chapter 8).
