## 4. Pilot Conversation Script Builder  
*Purpose: Prepare a natural, collaborative opening for your first pilot conversation. This is not a script you read verbatim—it’s a set of muscle‑memory prompts.*

**Opening Frame**  
*Set the context as an experiment that benefits both sides.*

*Example:* “I’d like to try something different with this engagement—a pricing structure that ties our fee directly to the result you need, rather than the hours it takes. My goal is to make the value we provide more transparent and to better align our incentives. I’m thinking of this as a collaborative pilot, and I’d welcome your honest feedback along the way.”

**Presenting the Fee**  
*Link the fee to the outcome and the risk you absorb, never to time.*

*Example:* “The fee for this outcome‑based engagement is $[VRR Fee]. That covers the complete delivery of what we’ve defined as success and reflects the risk we’re taking on. There won’t be any additional charges unless the scope changes by mutual agreement.”

*If the client hesitates, treat it as data, not rejection.*

*Example prompt:* “I’m curious to hear your initial reaction—what feels right, and what gives you pause? This is a pilot, so we can adjust the structure for future engagements based on what we both learn.”
