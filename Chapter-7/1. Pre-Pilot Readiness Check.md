## 1. Pre-Pilot Readiness Check

Before you pick an engagement, check whether you’re psychologically and structurally ready to run a pilot. Answer honestly; this is for your eyes only.

| Readiness Statement | Yes | Partially | No |
|---------------------|:--:|:---------:|:--:|
| I have a completed AI-susceptibility heatmap from Chapter 4 with at least one green or yellow zone service. | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| I have a VRR fee calculated for at least one engagement (Chapter 5). | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| I have a draft outcome statement and archetype selection for that engagement (Chapter 6). | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| I am willing to run a pilot that might not go perfectly, without interpreting it as a career failure. | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| I am prepared to have an explicit conversation with a client about an outcome-based fee, not just slip a new document into a renewal. | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| I accept that the pilot’s first goal is learning; fee maximization is secondary. | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |
| I’ve identified at least one client with whom I have enough trust to say, “I’d like to try something new with you and get your feedback.” | ☐ | ☐ | ☐ |

If you marked “No” on any, address that gap before you proceed. You don’t need perfection, but the pilot rests on a completed audit, a VRR number, and an outcome statement. Without those, you’re guessing.
