## 10. Key Concepts Summary Card  
*The pilot protocol’s essentials in a single glance.*

---

**THE 3‑C FILTER**  
- **Complexity:** Keep it simple. If you can’t write the outcome statement in two sentences, it’s too complex for a pilot.  
- **Client Openness:** Choose a client who trusts you deeply and is open to experiments.  
- **Containability:** Bound the scope, timeline, and financial exposure tightly.

**THE PILOT BLUEPRINT (One Page)**  
1. Outcome Definition & Success Criteria  
2. Financial Architecture (VRR fee, internal rationale, old‑model comparison)  
3. Value Assurance & Risk Allocation  
4. 90‑Day Milestone & Feedback Plan  
5. Conversation Prompts

**THE RAPID FEEDBACK LOOP**  
- Capture verbatims immediately after the initial conversation, at the midpoint check‑in, and in the final debrief.  
- Ask directly about the pricing model: “How is this fixed‑fee arrangement feeling?”

**POST‑ENGAGEMENT METRICS**  
- Realization rate, gross margin, client satisfaction, willingness to repeat, referral signals.  
- Hard data neutralizes “success theater.”

**COMMON TRAPS**  
1. **Overconfidence:** Don’t skip the pilot; don’t scale prematurely.  
2. **Scope Creep:** Define exclusions and never add work without a new outcome statement and fee.  
3. **Success Theater:** Evaluate on numbers and verbatims, not just warm feelings.

**THE GOLDEN RULE**  
*The pilot is not about proving you’re right. It’s about learning what your clients need to feel safe in a non‑hourly world—and then giving them exactly that.*

---

**Closing thought:** A single successful pilot is worth more than a hundred articles on value pricing. It’s your proof, your confidence, and your shield against the doubters—including the one in your own head. Run it with care, capture everything, and let the results speak. The retraining scripts in Chapter 8 will then help you take that proof and use it to open doors that would otherwise stay closed.
