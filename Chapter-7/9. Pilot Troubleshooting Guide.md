## 9. Pilot Troubleshooting Guide

Inevitably, something will go slightly sideways. Use this guide to course-correct without abandoning the model.

| Problem | Typical Symptom | Immediate Action | Longer-Term Fix |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|-----------------|
| Client demands hourly backup despite outcome package. | “I need to see the hours for our internal approval.” | Offer a good-faith resource estimate as context, not a billing basis. Reaffirm the outcome guarantee. | Revise your value assurance mechanism to be more prominent; strengthen Chapter 8 retraining scripts. |
| Scope creeps without fee adjustment. | Client adds requests mid-project without formal change. | Pause work immediately. “I’m happy to handle this, but it’s outside our defined outcome. Let’s agree on a revised outcome and fee.” | Build a change control clause into all future engagement letters. |
| Client expresses dissatisfaction with the fee at a milestone. | “This feels expensive for what we’ve seen so far.” | Reconnect to the outcome statement: “Let’s revisit what we agreed success looks like. The fee is for the result, and we’re on track to deliver it.” | Ensure the outcome definition is specific enough to be demonstrably valuable. |
| Pilot realization rate is worse than the old hourly model. | You collected the fee but your effective rate dropped when factoring in unplanned work. | Analyze whether the VRR fee underestimated the OS or overestimated efficiency. Adjust JI or RA for next time. | Treat this as a calibration win, not a failure. You learned where the formula needs tightening. |
| Client says yes in conversation but hesitates at signing. | Delay in returning signed engagement letter. | Follow up with a call, not an email. Ask directly: “Is there anything about the structure that’s causing hesitation? I want to address it.” | Possibly reduce pilot scope further or add a stronger assurance mechanism. |

*Record any problems and solutions in your Feedback Log. They are the raw material for your internal sell story.*
