## 8. Common Pilot Traps & Avoidance Checklist  
*Use this checklist before, during, and after your pilot to stay out of the three most damaging traps.*

**Trap 1: Overconfidence (“I can skip the pilot and go straight to scale”)**  
- [ ] I have explicitly limited this test to one or two engagements.  
- [ ] I have not sent mass communications or amended letters to all clients.  
- [ ] I have identified a mentor or trusted peer who can reality‑check my assumptions.

**Trap 2: Scope Creep During the Pilot**  
- [ ] The outcome definition includes clear exclusions.  
- [ ] I have a prepared response if the client asks to add work: “I’m glad you want to expand—let’s create a separate outcome statement and fee for that piece so we can keep this pilot clean.”  
- [ ] The 90‑day milestone plan includes a scope check‑in at the midpoint.  
- [ ] I commit to not saying “yes” to scope expansion without adjusting the fee in writing.

**Trap 3: Success Theater (declaring victory based on warm feelings without hard data)**  
- [ ] I have captured realization rate and margin data (see Metrics Summary).  
- [ ] I have documented client verbatims—positive, negative, and neutral.  
- [ ] I will not declare the pilot a success until the post‑engagement debrief is complete and the metrics are analyzed.  
- [ ] If the client is happy but realization rate dropped, I will treat that as a signal to adjust my VRR calibration.
