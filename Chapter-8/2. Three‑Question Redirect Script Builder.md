## 2. Three‑Question Redirect Script Builder  
*Purpose: Turn the most common invoice challenge into a collaborative value discovery. Customize this template for a specific client engagement you’ve recently completed or are about to complete.*

**Engagement / Client context:**  
____________________________________________________

**What the client might say to trigger the redirect (pick one or write your own):**  
- “How many hours did this take?”  
- “What’s the effective hourly rate here?”  
- “This looks expensive compared to our usual bills.”

**Your redirect script (fill in the blanks, then practice aloud):**

**Question 1: Acknowledge the outcome first.**  
*“Before we get into the mechanics, can we confirm that the outcome we agreed on was delivered to your satisfaction?”*

*If the client says yes:*  
*“Great. And just to put that in your own words—what did having that outcome secured actually mean for the business? In other words, what was at stake if this had gone wrong?”*

*If the client says no or is uncertain:* Pause. This is a performance conversation, not a pricing conversation. Address the gap before continuing. The script below assumes the outcome was met.

**Question 2: Let the client state the business impact.**  
*Listen. Don’t interrupt. Write down the exact dollar figure or risk description they use. If they ask you to provide the number, you can say:*  
*“Based on our discussions when we set this up, we estimated that about $[Outcome Significance from VRR] was at risk if the certification failed. Does that sound right to you?”*

**Client’s estimated impact (fill in after the conversation):**  
____________________________________________________

**Question 3: Ask the closing comparison question.**  
*“Given that outcome and the risk it neutralized, does the fee feel like a reasonable investment in that certainty?”*

*If they say yes:* the conversation is effectively over.  
*If they hesitate:* that’s valuable data. Use the Five Objections builder (Section 4) to address their specific concern.

**Practice note:** The power of this redirect is that you never mention hours, effort, or time. You lead the client to compare the fee to the risk, not the clock. Role‑play this until the sequence feels like a natural conversation, not a script.
