## 10. Key Concepts Summary Card  
*The retraining conversation architecture distilled into a portable reminder.*

---

**THE THREE‑QUESTION REDIRECT**  
1. *“Can we confirm the outcome was delivered to your satisfaction?”*  
2. *“What was at stake if this had gone wrong?”*  
3. *“Given that outcome and the risk it neutralized, does the fee feel like a reasonable investment?”*  

→ Never mention hours. Let the client compare the fee to the risk.

**THE FUTURE‑PACING CLOSE**  
*“For our next engagement, I’d like to use the same structure: define the outcome, agree on a fixed fee, no surprises. Does that sound like a better way to work together?”*  

→ Sets the new default without asking for a new commitment on the spot.

**THE FIVE OBJECTIONS (AND YOUR SYSTEM ANCHORS)**  
1. **“How do I know I’m not overpaying?”** → Anchor: VRR fee vs. risk transferred.  
2. **“Procurement needs hourly data.”** → Anchor: Offer a value summary, not a timesheet.  
3. **“What if the scope changes?”** → Anchor: Scope‑change protocol in the engagement letter.  
4. **“What if the outcome isn’t achieved?”** → Anchor: Value assurance clause.  
5. **“I’ve never seen this before.”** → Anchor: Pilot data and industry trends.

**THE GOLDEN RULE**  
*Your script is only as strong as the system behind it. When the audit, the VRR engine, the packaging, and the pilot data are in place, the retraining conversation stops being a negotiation and starts being a simple, honest reflection of the value you’ve already delivered.*
