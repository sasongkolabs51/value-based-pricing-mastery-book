## 5. Objection-to-Evidence Mapping Worksheet

This tool ensures you never have to invent a defense on the spot. For each objection, identify the specific system asset that backs up your response.

| Objection | System Asset I’ll Reference | Specific Number / Clause / Quote |
|-----------|-----------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Overpayment / no hours tracked | VRR engine: OS and RA | OS midpoint $____; fee as ___% of risk |
| Procurement demands hourly backup | Outcome packaging: value assurance, business case summary | Value assurance mechanism: ______________ |
| Scope change concerns | Engagement letter: change control clause | “Scope change requires mutual agreement on new outcome and fee before work begins.” |
| Outcome not achieved | Value assurance mechanism; risk allocation language | Refund/credit: ______% under specified conditions |
| “Never seen this before” | Pilot results; industry data | Pilot client feedback quote: “…” or “Pilot resulted in ____% realization rate and client willing to repeat.” |

Now, practice connecting each objection to its evidence source. When a client says “What if the scope changes?”, your brain should automatically route to the change control clause, not to an anxious internal calculation.
