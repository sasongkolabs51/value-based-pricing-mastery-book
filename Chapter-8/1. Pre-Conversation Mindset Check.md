## 1. Pre-Conversation Mindset Check

Before you practice a single script, name what might be holding you back. Tick any statement that resonates.

- [ ] I’m nervous that a client will ask for an hourly breakdown and I’ll freeze.
- [ ] I’ve never explicitly asked a client to describe the business impact of my work—it feels intrusive.
- [ ] When I think about saying “the fee is tied to the risk we absorbed,” I worry the client will laugh or push back hard.
- [ ] I’m afraid of sounding like a salesperson rather than a trusted professional.
- [ ] Part of me still believes the hourly model is the “honest” way to bill, and I’m not sure I can fully defend an alternative.
- [ ] I haven’t yet internalized my own VRR calculation well enough to explain it casually.
- [ ] I’ve been avoiding this conversation with a specific client because I don’t want to risk the relationship.

If you checked any of these, you’re exactly where Marcus was before his call—and exactly where this workbook meets you. The scripts that follow aren’t about becoming a slick negotiator; they’re about giving you the structure to let the system speak for itself.
