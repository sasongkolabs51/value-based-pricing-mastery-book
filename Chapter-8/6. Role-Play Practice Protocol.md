## 6. Role-Play Practice Protocol

Purpose: Move the scripts from head knowledge to conversational muscle memory. Find a colleague (ideally one also working through the book) and dedicate two 45-minute sessions.

### Session 1: Foundational Drills

1. **Three-Question Redirect only:** Your colleague plays a client who opens with “I looked at the invoice and I’m trying to figure out what I actually paid per hour.” You respond only with the three questions—no objections yet. Repeat three times, aiming for a natural, unhurried delivery.
2. **Objection handling isolation:** Your colleague picks one objection at a time from Section 4. You respond using your customized script. After each response, the colleague gives brief feedback: “Did that feel defensive?” “Did I believe the evidence?” Repeat each objection twice.
3. **Future-Pacing Close practice:** After successfully handling an objection, immediately deliver your Future-Pacing Close. Practice the transition from “Does that sound fair?” to “For our next piece of work…”

### Session 2: Complex Simulations

Now the colleague throws curveballs—objections in rapid sequence, interruptions, changing the subject. Your task: maintain the outcome frame throughout, use the redirect when appropriate, and close with the future-pacing.

**Example simulation flow:**
- Client: “This invoice seems high. What’s the effective hourly rate?”  
- You: Question 1 → Client confirms outcome.  
- You: Question 2 → Client describes impact but remains skeptical.  
- You: Question 3 → Client hesitates. “Maybe, but I’m still not sure how to compare this to other firms.”  
- You: Pivot to Objection 1 response (overpayment).  
- Client: “Okay, but my procurement team will still want an hourly breakdown.”  
- You: Objection 2 response.  
- Client: “What about the next project? Will you do it this way again?”  
- You: Future-Pacing Close.

After each simulation, pause and note: Which part felt most natural? Where did I revert to defending the hours? Record the session on your phone and review it later.
