## 5. Role‑Play Practice Guide & Script Skeleton  
*Purpose: Build muscle memory so the full conversation flows naturally under pressure.*

**What you’ll need:** A trusted colleague, 45–60 minutes, and a way to record (phone voice memo is fine).

**Step 1: Set the scene.**  
- You: the partner who has just delivered an outcome‑based engagement.  
- Your colleague: the client CFO, initially skeptical. They will open with: *“I got the invoice. Can you walk me through how you arrived at this fee? It seems high compared to what we’d have paid hourly.”*

**Step 2: Run the Three‑Question Redirect.**  
Use your customized script from Section 2. Do not read it—speak naturally. If you stumble, keep going. The recording will capture where you got stuck.

**Step 3: Let your colleague introduce objections.**  
They should pick from the five objections, in any order, and throw in a curveball like “But AI probably did most of the work, right?” (which is a variant of Objection 1). Use your responses from Section 4.

**Step 4: Transition to the Future‑Pacing Close.**  
After resolving the objections, smoothly move into your close from Section 3. The colleague can agree, hesitate, or ask for time to think—respond accordingly.

**Step 5: Switch roles and repeat.**  
This builds empathy and helps you hear how the conversation lands from the client’s side.

**Step 6: Listen to the recording together** (or solo) and fill out the Conversation Quality Scorecard (Section 6).
