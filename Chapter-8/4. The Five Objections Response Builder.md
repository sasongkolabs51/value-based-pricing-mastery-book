## 4. The Five Objections Response Builder

For each common objection, you’ll find a base response and space to adapt it with your own system evidence. Work through them all, even if you think a particular objection is unlikely. The goal is to have these responses available without conscious thought.

### Objection 1: “How do I know I’m not overpaying if you’re not tracking hours?”

**Base response script:**  
“That’s a fair question. The fee is based on the financial risk you transferred to us—in this case, about $____ in potential liability if [the outcome] had failed. The fee is roughly [X]% of that risk. If you’d prefer, we could have tracked hours and billed you an estimated $____ under the old model, but that would have left you exposed to the full risk without the certainty of a fixed price. Which feels like the better deal?”

### Objection 3: “What if the scope changes? How do we handle that without hours?”

**Base response script:**  
“Great question. In the engagement letter, we included a scope-change protocol: if the scope expands, we define a new outcome and agree on an adjusted fee before any extra work begins. That way you never get a surprise invoice, and we never work beyond what’s agreed. It’s actually cleaner than the hourly model, where scope creep can happen silently.”

### Objection 4: “What if the outcome isn’t achieved? Do we still pay?”

**Base response script:**  
“That depends on why. If we fail to deliver the defined outcome due to our error, the value assurance clause in the engagement letter specifies that [a portion of the fee is credited / we remediate at our cost]. If the outcome isn’t achieved because of factors outside our control that we agreed upon upfront, the fee stands because we still assumed the risk during the engagement period. It’s the same dynamic as insurance—you pay the premium even if your house doesn’t burn down, because you were protected.”

**Your version:** Insert the specifics of your value assurance mechanism (e.g., “15% of the fee is refundable if the certification fails for reasons within our control”).
