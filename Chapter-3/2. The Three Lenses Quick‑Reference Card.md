## 2. The Three Lenses Quick‑Reference Card  
*Keep this card visible while you work through the exercises. It distills the mental framework of the chapter into portable questions.*

---

**LENS 1: Replaceability**  
*What part of my current output could a competent professional, armed with GPT‑6, produce in under an hour?*  
- Looks for: pattern-matching, template-driven, high-data, low-context tasks.  
- Signals: tasks you used to assign to a junior who followed a checklist.  
- Mindset shift: “Those hours were never the value; they were the cost of production.”

**LENS 2: Judgment**  
*What does my client actually need that no machine can approximate?*  
- Three irreplaceable capabilities:  
  - **Contextual calibration** (regulator personalities, political undercurrents, unwritten history)  
  - **Decision ownership** (being the accountable professional who says “I advise you to proceed”)  
  - **Existential reassurance** (the human voice saying “I’ve seen this before, and the path is clear”)  
- Signals: any moment where a client won’t act on an AI analysis without your nod.

**LENS 3: Risk**  
*Where am I absorbing liability that the client would otherwise carry?*  
- AI incurs zero liability; you do. If a missed clause costs millions, you get sued, not the machine.  
- This risk assumption is a service with economic value, independent of time spent.  
- Signals: engagements where the client’s downside is large, specific, and transferrable to your firm.
