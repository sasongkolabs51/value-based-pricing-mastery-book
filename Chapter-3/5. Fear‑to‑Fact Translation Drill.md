## 5. Fear‑to‑Fact Translation Drill  
*Purpose: Break the loop of peer‑fueled anxiety by converting every scary statement into a fact‑based diagnostic.*

**Step 1: Find a trigger.**  
Pull up that Fishbowl thread (or any similar source—a LinkedIn post, a news article, a colleague’s worried comment) that recently unsettled you. Copy or summarize one specific claim below.

**Claim:**  
*“___”*

**Step 2: Map it to your snapshot.**  
Based on your Snapshot Worksheets, which quadrant(s) of your practice does this claim actually touch? Be specific. “This claim would apply to my Engagement A, components 2 and 4, which sit in the AI‑Susceptible zone. It does not touch components 1, 3, or 5, which are in the stronghold.”

---

**Step 3: Write a one‑sentence factual correction.**  
Use the language of the three lenses. For example: “The machine can draft the first‑cut report, but the liability for a missed $50M exposure still rests with us, and the board‑level synthesis requires contextual calibration no algorithm possesses.” Write yours:

---

**Drill reinforcement:** Do this once a day for a week with a different trigger each time. Over time, your brain will automatically route anxiety through the matrix and produce a factual correction instead of panic.
