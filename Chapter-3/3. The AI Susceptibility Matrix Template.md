## 3. The AI Susceptibility Matrix Template  
*Use this two‑axis map to plot every service component you list in the snapshot exercise.*

**Axes:**  
- **Horizontal axis:** Standardization & Data Dependency (Low → High)  
  - Low: unique, non‑codifiable, reliant on tacit knowledge.  
  - High: repeatable, rules‑based, data‑rich, well‑documented.  

- **Vertical axis:** Judgment Intensity & Risk Density (Low → High)  
  - High: demands context, ownership, reassurance, and carries significant liability for the firm.  
  - Low: objective, easily validated, minimal client‑side consequence if wrong.

**Quadrants:**

|  | **High Judgment / Risk** | **Low Judgment / Risk** |
|----------------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **High Standardization** | **Mixed Quadrant (Upper Right):** AI can accelerate, but human oversight & accountability remain critical. Pilot candidates live here. | **AI‑Susceptible Zone (Lower Right):** Pure process. Automate, productize, or price by subscription. |
| **Low Standardization** | **AI‑Proof Stronghold (Upper Left):** Your most durable value. Crisis response, novel strategy, board‑level counsel. Protect and price for outcomes. | **Commodity Plus (Lower Left):** Low standard but low stakes—likely small, bespoke tasks. Not a core threat, not a core margin driver. |

*You may draw this matrix on a whiteboard, in a notebook, or use a digital canvas. When plotting service components, place them according to where you rated them on the three lenses (Replaceability = inverse of Standardization, Judgment Intensity, Risk Density).*
