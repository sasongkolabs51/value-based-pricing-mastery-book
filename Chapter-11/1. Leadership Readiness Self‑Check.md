## 1. Leadership Readiness Self‑Check  
*Before you step onto the visible stage, take a quick, honest reading of where your leadership identity stands. Rate each statement 1 (Not at all) to 5 (Completely).*

| Statement | 1–5 |
|-----------|-----|
| I can tell the story of my own billable‑hour crisis—the specific moment that forced me to change—in under two minutes. |  |
| I have a clear, evidence‑backed narrative of the results my new pricing model has achieved (realization rates, client satisfaction, margin). |  |
| I’m willing to share anonymized results publicly, even if it attracts some skepticism. |  |
| I know at least two other partners in my firm who would join a Value Pricing Council if I asked. |  |
| I feel equipped to handle dismissive comments from competitors or skeptics without getting defensive. |  |
| I have a regular practice—even if informal—of reflecting on my professional journey and adjusting my course. |  |
| I’m genuinely excited, not just obligated, by the idea of mentoring others through this transition. |  |

**Interpreting your scores:**  
- If you scored low on statements 1–3, the Pricing Perspective builder (Section 2) will help you crystallize your narrative.  
- Low scores on 4–5 are normal—this chapter exists to give you the institutional and personal tools to build that confidence.  
- Statement 7 is the heart of leadership. If you feel obligation more than excitement, revisit your Value Architect manifesto (Chapter 2) and reconnect with why you started this journey.
