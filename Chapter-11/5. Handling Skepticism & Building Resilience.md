## 5. Handling Skepticism & Building Resilience

Leadership draws fire. Prepare your responses and your recovery system.

### External Skepticism Response Scripts

**Comment you might hear:** *“Value pricing is just a fad—clients will always revert to hours.”*  
**Your response:** *“I understand the skepticism—I used to think the same. But I’ve been running this model for [X months], and my realization rates are actually higher now, and client feedback is better. The shift is already happening; I’m just sharing what’s working so others can try it without the trial and error.”*

**Comment:** *“This only works for niche advisory firms, not high-volume practices.”*  
**Your response:** *“The system includes a diagnostic that identifies which services are ready for outcome pricing and which are better suited to subscription or automation. It’s not all-or-nothing. I’d be happy to walk through how it could apply to your situation.”*

### The Doubt Detox Log

Even leaders have moments of panic. When the old billable-hour voice returns, capture and reframe it.

| Date | Situation (what triggered doubt) | The thought (“I’m overcharging,” “This will fail,” etc.) | System evidence that refutes it (pilot data, client quote, manifesto) | Reframe (what’s true) |
|------|----------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------|
| | | | | |
| | | | | |

**Example:**  
- *Situation:* Client’s CFO raised an eyebrow at the fixed fee.  
- *Doubt:* “Maybe I should have just billed hours—this feels greedy.”  
- *Evidence:* The same CFO later said, “This gave us budget certainty and saved us from a potential $50 million penalty.”  
- *Reframe:* “I’m not charging for time; I’m charging for the removal of a risk they acknowledged is real.”
