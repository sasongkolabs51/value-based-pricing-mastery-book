## 1. Pre-Leadership Mindset Check

Visible leadership can feel riskier than private mastery. Name the hesitations before you work through them. Tick any that resonate.

- [ ] I’m reluctant to put myself forward as a “Pricing Pioneer”—it feels self-promotional.
- [ ] I worry that sharing my results publicly will invite criticism from competitors or traditionalists.
- [ ] I haven’t carved out time to write or speak; client work always takes priority.
- [ ] The idea of leading a firm-wide council or being the public face of this change makes me feel exposed.
- [ ] Sometimes I still have moments where I wonder if I’ll be “found out”—that the model only worked because of luck.
- [ ] I’ve never thought of myself as an industry thought leader; I’m just a practitioner who figured something out.
- [ ] I’m not sure how to handle a partner who actively undermines the transition without creating conflict.

If you ticked any of these, you’re in the right place. Every leader starts with these doubts. The difference is that leaders act despite them, and this workbook turns those impulses into concrete actions that make the discomfort manageable.
