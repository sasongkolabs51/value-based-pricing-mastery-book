## 1. Pre-Engine Mindset Check

Before you calculate, surface the pricing anxiety that Eleanor felt. Check any that apply.

- [ ] I’m afraid that if I price on outcomes, the number will be too high and scare the client away.
- [ ] I don’t fully trust my own estimate of what an outcome is worth to a client.
- [ ] I worry that the client will demand to see the underlying “time and materials” breakdown, and I won’t have one.
- [ ] Part of me still believes hours are the fairest way to charge—for me and for the client.
- [ ] I’ve never had a structured conversation with a client about the dollar value of the risk they face.
- [ ] I suspect my judgment is valuable, but I’m not sure how to put a number on it.
- [ ] The idea of a “formula” for pricing feels cold or impersonal compared to a relationship-based discussion.

If you checked any of these, you’re not broken—you’re normal. The VRR engine exists to give you a starting number and the language to discuss it. It’s a compass, not a cage.
