## 4. Pushback Rehearsal Script Builder  
*Prepare for the most common client objections by scripting and practicing your redirects.*

---

**Objection 1: “Your fee seems high compared to what we’ve paid before.”**

- **Don’t say:** “Well, if you look at the hours we used to spend…”  
- **Redirect:** Acknowledge the comparison, then pivot to the financial exposure.  
- *Your script:* “I understand that it’s a different structure than what we’ve used in the past. But when you compare this fee to the $[OS Midpoint] in value at risk, it represents less than [X]% to secure certainty. Previously, you were paying for time, not for a guaranteed outcome. Now you’re buying the outcome itself—and the liability that comes with it.”

**Objection 2: “Can you show me the hours behind this?”**

- **Don’t say:** “Sure, here’s a timesheet estimate.”  
- **Redirect:** Refocus on the risk transfer and the irrelevance of hours.  
- *Your script:* “I’m happy to walk you through the value drivers, but breaking this down by hours misses the point. You’re not buying our minutes—you’re buying our commitment that this risk is contained. The fee reflects the size of the risk we’re absorbing on your behalf, not the length of time it takes to neutralize it. Whether it takes two days or two weeks, the protection you get is the same.”

**Objection 3: “What if you finish faster because of AI? Shouldn’t that lower the price?”**

- **Don’t say:** “We’ll discount it.”  
- **Redirect:** Position speed as a client benefit that doesn’t diminish the risk.  
- *Your script:* “The speed is an advantage for you—you get the outcome and the certainty sooner. But the risk we’re taking on doesn’t shrink just because we’re more efficient. In fact, our ability to deliver that certainty faster is a result of our expertise and technology. The price reflects the value of the outcome and the accountability we bear, not the stopwatch.”

**Practice plan:**  
- Read each script aloud three times.  
- Ask a colleague (or even your phone’s voice recorder) to play the client and give the objection. Respond naturally, using your script as a foundation but not reading it verbatim.  
- Do this until you no longer feel defensive. The goal is conviction, not recitation.
