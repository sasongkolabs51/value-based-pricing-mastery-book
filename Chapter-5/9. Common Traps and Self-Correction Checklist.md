## 9. Common Traps and Self-Correction Checklist

Before finalizing any VRR proposal, run through these checks.

- [ ] **Overinflated OS?** Can I point to a client document or statement that supports the OS number? If not, I’ve either guessed or projected. Label it as an estimate and validate with client.
- [ ] **Undercalibrated JI?** Did I reduce the multiplier because I felt uncomfortable, not because the work is less judgment-intensive? Revisit Disaster Portfolio. The JI should reflect reality, not anxiety.
- [ ] **Commoditized service?** Is this service in my Susceptible quadrant? If so, VRR isn’t the right tool. Shift to subscription or automation pricing.
- [ ] **RA too aggressive?** Does my firm’s general counsel or risk partner agree that we’re actually assuming the liability level implied? If not, adjust RA down and note that the fee reflects a lower risk transfer.
- [ ] **Fee shock?** Did I compare the VRR fee only to the old hourly estimate, not the client’s at-risk value? Remember, the client’s benchmark is the cost of the problem, not the cost of your time.
- [ ] **Proposal language still contains time references?** Search for words like “hours,” “time,” “effort,” “weeks,” “team size.” Replace them with outcome and risk language.
