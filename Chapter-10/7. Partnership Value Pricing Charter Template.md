**PARTNERSHIP VALUE PRICING CHARTER**  
*[Firm Name]*  
*Effective Date: ______________*

**Preamble**  
The partnership acknowledges that the billable hour model is under structural pressure from AI commoditization, client demand for value alignment, and declining realization rates. This charter establishes a governed, opt-in pilot to develop and evaluate outcome-based pricing as a sustainable alternative.

**Principles**  
- Client value and risk absorption, not time spent, should be the primary basis for pricing professional services.  
- AI-augmented efficiency must flow to firm margin and client value, not to automatic discounts.  
- Partner compensation should reward outcomes, realization, and client relationships.  
- The transition will be evidence-based, opt-in, and reviewed before any firm-wide mandate.

**Value Pricing Council**  
A council is established, comprising [3–5] partners, to:  
- Oversee the pilot program and maintain quality standards.  
- Capture and share pilot data, templates, and best practices.  
- Mentor partners entering the pilot.  
- Report quarterly to the Executive Committee on pilot progress.

**Pilot Program**  
- Duration: 12 months from charter adoption.  
- Participation: Voluntary. Any partner may opt in by notifying the Council and committing to at least one outcome-based engagement.  
- Compensation: Pilot participants will be compensated under the Partner Compensation Pilot structure approved by the Executive Committee (attached).  
- Support: The Council will provide templates, coaching, and client conversation scripts to pilot participants.

**Review and Decision**  
At the conclusion of the pilot period, the Council will present a comprehensive report to the partnership, including: aggregated realization rates, margin comparisons, client satisfaction data, talent retention metrics, and partner feedback. The partnership will vote on whether to adopt outcome-based pricing as a standard offering, expand the pilot, or discontinue.
