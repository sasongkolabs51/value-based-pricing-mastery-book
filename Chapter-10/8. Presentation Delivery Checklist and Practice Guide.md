## 8. Presentation Delivery Checklist and Practice Guide

Use this checklist to prepare for the actual presentation. Practice is not optional; the partnership can smell a rehearsal that never happened.

### Before the Presentation

- [ ] 10-slide deck finalized, with data sources verified and client quotes anonymized where needed.
- [ ] Handouts prepared: one-page charter, compensation pilot summary, and optionally, a summary of your pilot results.
- [ ] Political map updated: early adopters briefed and confirmed as visible supporters in the room.
- [ ] Executive committee sponsor aligned and knows the specific ask.
- [ ] Anticipated objections mapped to responses (Section 5), with relevant evidence at hand (pilot data, compensation design, etc.).
- [ ] Dry run completed with a trusted colleague who played “skeptical partner.” Incorporate their feedback.
- [ ] The “ask” is crystal clear—you can state it in one sentence without looking at notes.

### During the Presentation

- [ ] Open with the burning platform, not the solution. Let the room feel the urgency before you offer the escape.
- [ ] Use your own pilot story as the emotional center—numbers matter, but a partner’s lived experience is what changes minds.
- [ ] When challenged, redirect to evidence, not argument: “That’s a fair question. Here’s what the data showed when we tested that…”
- [ ] Keep the compensation slide simple. Partners will drill into details later; first they need to trust that their draws are safe.
- [ ] End with a specific, time-bound ask. “I’m requesting that we approve this charter today, so we can start the pilot next month and review results in 12 months.”

### After the Presentation

- [ ] Follow up within 24 hours with the charter and any promised materials.
- [ ] Schedule the first Value Pricing Council meeting before you leave the room (or within the week).
- [ ] Personally thank early adopters who spoke up, and check in with fence-sitters who had questions.
- [ ] Start tracking commitments: who will join the pilot? What’s their first engagement?
