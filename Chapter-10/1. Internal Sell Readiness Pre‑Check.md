## 1. Internal Sell Readiness Pre‑Check  
*Before building your case, take a quick reading of where your firm’s readiness and your own positioning stand. Rate each statement 1 (Not at all) to 5 (Completely).*

| Statement | 1–5 |
|-----------|-----|
| I have clear pilot data showing improved realization rates and client satisfaction from at least one outcome‑based engagement. |  |
| I understand my firm’s current overall realization rate trajectory and have a sense of profit‑per‑partner trends. |  |
| I can name at least two other partners who are frustrated with the billable hour and might support a transition. |  |
| I know which partners are likely to resist and what their specific objections will be. |  |
| I have a concrete proposal for how partner compensation could work under an outcome‑based model (even if only for a pilot period). |  |
| I feel equipped to answer the question “Will this hurt my draw?” with data, not just philosophy. |  |
| I’m willing to invest political capital to get this approved, including having hard one‑on‑one conversations before the big meeting. |  |

**Interpreting your scores:**  
- If you scored low on statements 1–3, you may need to complete at least one robust pilot and gather the data before going firm‑wide.  
- Low scores on 4–6 signal gaps in your political map and compensation proposal—the tools in this chapter will fill those gaps.  
- Statement 7 is about commitment. The internal sell is not a single presentation; it’s a campaign. Be honest about your willingness to run it.
