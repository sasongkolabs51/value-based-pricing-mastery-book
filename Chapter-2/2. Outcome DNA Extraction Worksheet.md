## 2. Outcome DNA Extraction Worksheet

This is the forensic dig that separates your true worth from process-level activity. It will reveal the three outcomes only you produce—your Outcome DNA. You’ll need your project records, your memory, and about 45 uninterrupted minutes.

### Part A: Engagement Harvest
List the most significant client engagements from the last five to seven years. Don’t worry about completeness; focus on the ones where you made a real difference. For each, write the **primary business outcome**, stripping away all process and time language. Example: Instead of “Conducted a 12-week compliance review,” write “Resolved a multi-agency investigation without fines or reputational damage.”

### Part B: Pattern Recognition
Review your Outcome column. Circle or highlight outcomes that share a similar DNA—same type of risk neutralized, same type of value created, same type of client impact. Use a highlighter or write keywords like “regulatory avoidance,” “deal rescue,” “penalty reduction,” “market access,” etc.

### Part C: Crystallizing Your Outcome DNA
Distill your answers into three crisp, client-facing outcome statements. Use this format:  
*“I [strong verb] [specific client type/problem] by [unique method/judgment], resulting in [quantified result].”*

Example for an M&A tax partner:
1. “I eliminate post-acquisition tax surprises for private equity buyers by designing deal structures that hold up under audit, preserving an average of $8M per transaction in negotiated value.”
2. “I secure tax-free treatment for cross-border reorganizations that competitors deem impossible, enabling market entry without incremental tax drag.”
3. “I protect C-suite officers from personal tax liability during exit events, converting potential six-figure exposure into zero.”

*These are now your scarce, non-commoditizable assets.* They cannot be reduced to hours. They are the bedrock of your Value Architect identity.
