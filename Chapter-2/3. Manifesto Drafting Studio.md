## 3. Manifesto Drafting Studio

Your manifesto is a one-sentence internal compass. It’s not for your website bio; it’s for you. Following the chapter’s structure—*“I [transformational verb] [specific client type] to [measurable business outcome] by [unique capability]”*—generate five candidates below. Push yourself. Use verbs with weight: *protect, secure, accelerate, neutralize, unlock, defend, transform, de-risk, enable, rescue.* Keep each candidate under 25 words.

**Candidate 1:**  
______________________________________________________________________  
**Candidate 2:**  
______________________________________________________________________  
**Candidate 3:**  
______________________________________________________________________  
**Candidate 4:**  
______________________________________________________________________  
**Candidate 5:**  
______________________________________________________________________  

Now read each aloud. Which one straightens your spine? Which feels most true to the outcomes you uncovered in your DNA? Take that best candidate and refine it further:

**Refined Manifesto (under 25 words):**  
______________________________________________________________________  

Example: *“I protect industrial executives from criminal liability and operational shutdown by reframing technical evidence into regulatory victory.”* (17 words)

When you’ve landed on it, write it on a card or sticky note and place it where you’ll see it daily—laptop edge, bathroom mirror, notebook cover. This is your new identity’s heartbeat.
