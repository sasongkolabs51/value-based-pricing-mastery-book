## 7. Conversation Scripts: When the Old Identity Tries to Take Over

In high-stakes moments, your brain will default to the “trusted advisor” script. Keep these alternative phrases handy. Practice them aloud until they feel natural.

| Old Identity Phrase | Value Architect Reframe |
|---------------------|------------------------|
| “We can handle that for you; our hourly rate is…” | “We’ll design an engagement around ensuring [specific outcome]. Our fee will align with the value of that result.” |
| “This project typically takes X hours, so the budget would be around…” | “Based on the outcomes you’ve described, we can structure a fee that reflects the business risk we’ll be removing, regardless of the time required.” |
| “I’ll send you a timesheet with the invoice.” | “I’ll send you a summary of the outcomes achieved against the metrics we set together.” |
| “I’m a tax partner / M&A lawyer / consultant.” | “I protect [client type] from [specific risk], ensuring they can [business outcome].” |
| “I’d love to be your trusted advisor.” | “My goal is to be the architect of your [outcome X]—and I’ll put my fee against that result.” |
| “We can do a fixed fee if you prefer.” | “The default model is outcome-based, because that’s where our interests align.” |

Select two that feel most relevant and rehearse them with a colleague or in front of a mirror. The shift isn’t in the words alone; it’s in the pause you take before speaking, choosing the Value Architect lane.
