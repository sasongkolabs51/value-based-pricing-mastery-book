## 3. Invoice Value Narrative Builder  
*Purpose: Transform a questioned (or question-prone) invoice into a one-page impact story with zero time references.*

**Choose one recent invoice** that a client challenged, or one you fear they might challenge. If none exists, pick an invoice that makes you inwardly wince because the hours don’t feel like they match the value.

**Invoice description (project name, client, amount):**  

---

**Step 1: Identify the business outcome.**  
What was the specific, tangible result the client got from this work? Don’t say “we reviewed contracts.” Say “we ensured the merger closed on time without regulatory entanglements.” Be concrete.

---

**Step 2: Quantify the risk avoided.**  
What would have happened if this work hadn’t been done—or had been done poorly? Think cost of delay, penalty, litigation, loss of a deal, compliance breach, reputation damage. Put a dollar figure or a clear consequence here.

---

**Step 3: Frame the value story.**  
Write a one-page narrative (three to four paragraphs) that explains the fee entirely through the lens of the outcome delivered. Use language a CFO would recognize: ROI, risk mitigation, speed to certainty, capital preserved. Do not mention a single hour, timesheet, or effort metric. The narrative should answer: “Here’s what we achieved, here’s what was at stake, and this fee secured that outcome.”

*Start drafting here (use the back of the page or extra sheet if needed):*  

---

**Step 4: Practice delivering it.**  
Read the narrative aloud once. Then, record yourself on your phone and listen back. Circle any phrases that still smell like “hours” and rewrite them. Repeat until the story feels natural, not like you’re defending a timesheet.

**Reflection:** After practicing, how do you feel about that invoice now? What shifted?
