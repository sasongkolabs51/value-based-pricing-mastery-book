## 1. Pre-Design Mindset Check

Before you start redesigning workflows, surface the hidden assumptions that keep the old pyramid standing. Tick any that feel true.

- [ ] I still think of my team’s value in terms of how many hours they bill, not the outcomes they help create.
- [ ] When I hear “AI can do that faster,” a small part of me worries about losing control over quality.
- [ ] I’m not sure how to evaluate a senior manager’s performance if I can’t point to their utilization rate.
- [ ] I worry that shifting compensation away from billable hours will demotivate my best people.
- [ ] I’ve never mapped out exactly who does what in a service line—it’s just “how we’ve always done it.”
- [ ] The idea of a “client-embedded” role feels like it would add cost, not create value.
- [ ] I’ve never calculated the actual margin on an engagement after accounting for all team costs and AI tooling.

If you ticked any, you’re holding a perfectly normal set of pyramid reflexes. This workbook replaces them with a structure that rewards speed, judgment, and client outcomes—and the first step is seeing your current workflow clearly.
