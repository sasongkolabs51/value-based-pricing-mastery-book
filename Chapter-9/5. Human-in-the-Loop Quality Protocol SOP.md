## 5. Human-in-the-Loop Quality Protocol SOP

This standard operating procedure ensures that AI-augmented work meets the outcome and liability standards your VRR fee promises. Adapt this template to your service line.

### Checkpoint 1: AI Output Validation

**Performed by:** AI-Augmented Analysis Professional  
**When:** Immediately after receiving AI-generated output (draft analysis, data synthesis, etc.)  
**Criteria:**
- Factual accuracy: Are all data points, citations, and regulatory references correct?  
- Contextual relevance: Does the output address the specific client situation, not just the general topic?  
- Completeness: Does the output cover all elements of the outcome scope?  

**Action:**  
- Correct any factual errors.  
- Add missing elements by adjusting AI prompts or supplementing manually.  
- If the output is fundamentally unsuitable, reject and re-prompt with refined parameters.  
**Documentation:** Record error types and frequency in the Quality Log (template below). This data feeds prompt refinement and AI tool selection.

### Checkpoint 2: Judgment Overlay

**Performed by:** AI-Augmented Analysis Professional (with escalation to Strategic Oversight as needed)  
**When:** After validated AI output is in hand, before synthesis.  
**Criteria:**
- Risk calibration: Does the AI’s risk rating align with the known regulatory/political/industry context? Where does the professional’s experience suggest a different weighting?  
- “Unknown unknowns”: Are there factors the AI could not have known (unwritten agency preferences, personality dynamics, recent events not in the training data) that change the analysis?  
- Client-specific nuance: Does the client’s risk appetite, history, or internal politics require a different interpretation?  

**Action:**  
- Overlay judgment annotations directly onto the AI output.  
- Flag any areas where the AI recommendation is overridden and explain why.  
- Escalate to Strategic Oversight if the override could significantly change the outcome or risk profile.  

### Checkpoint 3: Partner Certification

**Performed by:** Strategic Oversight (Partner)  
**When:** Before delivery to client.  
**Criteria:**
- The deliverable meets the outcome definition in the engagement letter.  
- All judgment overrides are reviewed and approved.  
- The risk allocation language from the engagement letter is consistent with what is being certified.  
- Any limitations or assumptions are clearly stated.  

**Action:**  
- Sign off on the deliverable, attaching the firm’s liability.  
- If not certifiable, return to the appropriate layer for revision with specific instructions.
