## 5. Human‑in‑the‑Loop Quality Protocol Template  
*Purpose: Document a repeatable SOP for the three checkpoints so that quality is consistent and liability is clearly assigned.*

**SOP Owner:** [Name]  
**Last Updated:** [Date]  
**Service Line(s):** [List]

**Checkpoint 1: AI Output Validation**  
- **Responsible Role:** AI‑Augmented Analysis professional  
- **Timing:** Immediately after AI generates initial output  
- **Checklist:**  
  - [ ] Factual accuracy: Cross‑check all cited regulations, figures, and dates against primary sources.  
  - [ ] Completeness: Verify output addresses every element of the outcome scope; flag missing sections.  
  - [ ] Contextual relevance: Confirm the analysis applies to the client’s specific jurisdiction, industry, and circumstances.  
- **Action on Failure:** Correct errors directly in the working document. Log the error type in the Quality Log (see below) for trend analysis. If error pattern suggests prompt flaw, update prompt library.  
- **Sign‑off:** Initial and date in the working document when complete.

**Checkpoint 2: Judgment Overlay**  
- **Responsible Role:** AI‑Augmented Analysis professional (may be a different person from CP1, depending on team structure)  
- **Timing:** After CP1 sign‑off, before delivering to partner for certification  
- **Checklist:**  
  - [ ] Contextual calibration: Adjust risk ratings, conclusions, or recommendations based on unwritten factors (regulator tendencies, political climate, client risk appetite).  
  - [ ] Edge case identification: Note any scenarios where AI confidence was low or where the standard rule may not apply.  
  - [ ] Documentation: Briefly record the rationale for any judgment override in a comment or appendix.  
- **Action on Failure (if a judgment call is later found inadequate):** Log in Quality Log. Discuss with partner to refine judgment guidelines.  
- **Sign‑off:** Initial and date.

**Checkpoint 3: Partner Certification**  
- **Responsible Role:** Strategic Oversight (Partner)  
- **Timing:** Before client delivery  
- **Checklist:**  
  - [ ] Overall quality review: Does the deliverable meet the outcome criteria defined in the engagement letter?  
  - [ ] Risk assumption verification: Am I willing to attach my firm’s liability to this deliverable?  
  - [ ] Client readiness: Is the deliverable presented in a way that reinforces the outcome narrative and the value we provided?  
- **Action on Failure:** Return to AI‑Augmented Analysis with specific, documented feedback. If rework is minor, partner may adjust directly but must communicate the change to the team.  
- **Sign‑off:** Final signature on deliverable.
