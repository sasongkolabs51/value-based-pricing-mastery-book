## 4. Transition Heatmap Builder  
*Now transform your scored list into a visual strategy map. This is your new dashboard.*

**Step 1: Prepare the grid.**  
On a blank page (or spreadsheet), draw a two‑axis grid:
- **X‑axis:** AI‑Susceptibility Composite Score (1 to 5, left to right). 1 = Highly Durable, 5 = Highly Susceptible.  
- **Y‑axis:** Annual Revenue Contribution (low to high, bottom to top). Use actual dollars or percentage of book.

**Step 2: Plot each service line.**  
Mark each service with a dot and label it. If revenue is similar for many small lines, group them if they share similar susceptibility scores—but only if truly comparable.

**Step 3: Divide into four strategic zones.**  
Draw dividing lines at the midpoint of each axis (score 3 on susceptibility, median revenue point for your practice). This creates four quadrants:

- **Red Zone (Upper Right):** High Susceptibility + High Revenue. *Urgent transition candidates.* These services are actively bleeding realization rates. They need a new pricing model (automation, subscription, fixed fee) immediately.  
- **Yellow Zone (Lower Right):** High Susceptibility + Low Revenue. *Candidates for aggressive automation or divestiture.* If they’re not strategically important, consider sunsetting or bundling them into higher-value offerings.  
- **Green Zone (Upper Left):** Low Susceptibility + High Revenue. *Your value‑pricing strongholds.* Protect these. Expand them. They can anchor outcome-based pricing with minimal client resistance.  
- **Blue Zone (Lower Left):** Low Susceptibility + Low Revenue. *Niche specialties.* Nurture or cross-sell, but don’t bet the firm on scaling them unless you see a clear path.

**Step 4: Annotate the heatmap.**  
Next to each dot, jot down the dominant factor driving its susceptibility (e.g., “high standardization,” “client perception lag”). This annotation will guide your specific transition tactics later.

**What to do with the map:**  
- Red zone items? You’ll address them with urgency in the pricing chapters ahead.  
- Green zone items? They become your immediate value‑pricing pilots and the core of your VRR engine.
