## 5. Composite Scoring and Heatmap Construction

### Step 1: Choose Your Weighting
You can use a simple average (each factor = 20%) or a weighted approach. For most practices, judgment requirement and risk transfer deserve higher weight because they are the strongest AI-proofing forces. A recommended starting weighting:

- Judgment Requirement: 30%
- Risk Transfer: 30%
- Standardization: 15%
- Data Dependency: 15%
- Client Perception: 10%

### Step 3: Build the Transition Heatmap
Plot each service line on a grid. The x-axis is the Composite AI-Susceptibility Score (1 = durable, 5 = highly susceptible). The y-axis is the service line’s annual revenue contribution (or % of total revenue). The resulting chart divides into four zones.

```
High Revenue |  URGENT TRANSITION   |   PROTECT & EXPAND
             |  (High Suscept,      |   (Low Suscept,
             |   High Revenue)      |    High Revenue)
             |                      |
             |----------------------|-----------------------
             |  AUTOMATE / DIVEST   |   NURTURE / CROSS-SELL
Low Revenue  |  (High Suscept,      |   (Low Suscept,
             |   Low Revenue)       |    Low Revenue)
             |______________________|________________________
                High Suscept (5)          Low Suscept (1)
                     AI-Susceptibility Score
```
