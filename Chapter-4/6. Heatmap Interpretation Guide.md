## 6. Heatmap Interpretation Guide

Use this guide to read your heatmap and set initial strategies.

**Red Zone: High Susceptibility, High Revenue**  
These are the services currently bleeding realization rates. The hours have compressed, but the fee structure hasn't. They are your most urgent transition candidates. You need to either (a) move them to a value-pricing model that prices the judgment/risk pieces if those exist, or (b) radically restructure delivery around AI to create a new profit engine (e.g., subscription). Don't ignore these—they're the hole in your boat.

**Yellow Zone: High Susceptibility, Low Revenue**  
Low-hanging fruit. These services are already small; AI will only shrink them further. Options: (1) Aggressively automate and offer as a low-cost, high-volume subscription or self-serve product; (2) bundle them into larger engagements where they become a value-add rather than a standalone sale; (3) stop offering them outright if they don't support a strategic client relationship. Do not spend your scarce value-pricing energy here; these are efficiency plays.

**Green Zone: Low Susceptibility, High Revenue**  
Your stronghold. These services are built on judgment, risk transfer, and client relationships that AI cannot replicate. Protect them by moving to outcome-based fees immediately. They are your proof points for the new model. Expand them—these are where you should invest marketing, thought leadership, and team development. Every percentage of revenue you shift into this zone increases your firm's resilience.

**Blue Zone: Low Susceptibility, Low Revenue**  
Niche specialties. They're durable but small. Decide whether they serve as a pipeline to larger green-zone work, a credential that supports your reputation, or a passion project you're willing to sustain. If none of the above, consider spinning them off or discontinuing them to free capacity for green-zone growth.
