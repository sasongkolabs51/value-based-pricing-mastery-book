## 3. The 5‑Factor Scoring Rubric Quick‑Reference  
*Use this while filling in the worksheet. It’s the precise language from the chapter, condensed for rapid consultation.*

**1. STANDARDIZATION**  
- **1 (Highly Durable):** Entirely bespoke, requiring novel analysis every time.  
- **3 (Moderate):** Follows a general framework but needs significant customization.  
- **5 (Highly Susceptible):** Fully templated, with clear decision trees and predictable outputs.

**2. DATA DEPENDENCY**  
- **1 (Highly Durable):** All insight comes from proprietary, non-public sources or personal experience.  
- **3 (Moderate):** Mix of public data and proprietary contextual knowledge.  
- **5 (Highly Susceptible):** All relevant data is publicly available, structured, and easily ingested by AI.

**3. JUDGMENT REQUIREMENT**  
- **1 (Highly Durable):** Requires irreplaceable human judgment, contextual calibration, and existential reassurance at every stage.  
- **3 (Moderate):** Involves complex analysis but rules are largely defined and adaptable by AI.  
- **5 (Highly Susceptible):** Minimal judgment required; purely process-driven and rule-based.

**4. RISK TRANSFER**  
- **1 (Highly Durable):** Your firm assumes full liability and regulatory risk for the work product.  
- **3 (Moderate):** Shared liability; you advise, client makes the final risk-bearing decision.  
- **5 (Highly Susceptible):** Client bears all risk regardless of your work product (e.g., pure training).

**5. CLIENT PERCEPTION**  
- **1 (Highly Durable):** Clients already expect and accept fixed-fee or value-based arrangements.  
- **3 (Moderate):** Clients are open to discussion but currently default to hourly expectations.  
- **5 (Highly Susceptible):** Clients would be deeply skeptical of non-hourly billing and demand timesheets.

*Note: A low score (1) means high durability and low AI susceptibility. A high score (5) means high susceptibility. The composite score is an inverse measure of pricing power under the old model.*
