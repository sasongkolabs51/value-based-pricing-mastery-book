## 8. Bias Checks and Peer Review Prompts

The most common audit errors are over-scoring judgment requirement (pride of authorship) and under-scoring client perception (because you’ve never discussed pricing openly). Use these prompts to self-correct, and if possible, share them with a trusted peer who can review a few of your scores.

**Self-check questions (ask for each service line):**

- “If a competitor with half my experience but access to the same AI produced this work, would the client notice the difference in the output, or only in the brand?”  
- “When was the last time a client specifically praised this service as something they couldn’t get anywhere else?”  
- “If I stopped doing this service tomorrow, would the client replace me with another firm at a similar hourly rate, or would they struggle to find a substitute?”  
- “Have I ever had a client push back on the fee for this type of work, and if so, did my defense rely on hours or on outcome?”

**Peer review request script:**  
*“I’m doing an internal audit of my practice to understand where AI might change our pricing model. Could you review my scores on three service lines and tell me if you think I’m being too generous on any factor? I’m especially worried I might over-rate the judgment component because it’s my baby. I need an outside view.”*
