## 2. Service Line Inventory & 5‑Factor Scoring Worksheet  
*This is the core of the audit. Be granular—lump services only if they truly share the same risk profile. Add rows as needed.*

**Instructions:**  
1. List every distinct service line you offer. Think deliverables, not practice areas. “Regulatory advisory” might break into “new regulation impact assessment,” “compliance program design,” “enforcement defense representation,” and “annual compliance health check.”  
2. For each service line, score all five factors on the 1–5 scale (1 = Highly Durable / low susceptibility, 5 = Highly Susceptible). Use the scoring rubric (Section 3) as your guide.  
3. Record the approximate annual revenue contribution of each service line (in thousands, or as a percentage of your book).  
4. Calculate a composite AI-Susceptibility Score. A simple average works, or weight Judgment Requirement and Risk Transfer at 30% each and the other three at ~13.3% if you prefer more precision. Note your weighting method.

**After scoring, reflect:**  
- Which service lines surprised you—scoring higher (more susceptible) than you’d assumed?  
- Are there any services you initially wanted to protect but the scores now suggest are better candidates for automation or subscription?  
- Which lines carry the highest revenue and lowest susceptibility? Those are your strongholds.
