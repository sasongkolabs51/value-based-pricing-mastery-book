## 1. Perpetual Readiness Self‑Check  
*Before you set up your quarterly loops, take a quick reading of how prepared you feel to keep evolving. Rate each statement 1 (Not at all) to 5 (Completely).*

| Statement | 1–5 |
|-----------|-----|
| I have a clear idea of which AI developments (tools, models, client-side changes) could affect my service lines in the next 12 months. |  |
| I’ve already set aside time—even if informal—to review my pricing model periodically. |  |
| I can quickly re-score a service line’s AI susceptibility if a new tool emerges. |  |
| I have a way to fund my own learning and experimentation (conferences, tool subscriptions, protected time) that doesn’t feel like a guilty trade-off against billable work. |  |
| I actively share what I’m learning with at least one colleague or mentee. |  |
| I can articulate, in a sentence or two, the legacy I want to leave in my practice and my profession. |  |
| I’m genuinely energized, not exhausted, by the idea of continually refining my model. |  |

**Interpreting your responses:**  
- Low scores on statements 2, 4, and 7 suggest you’ll benefit most from the Innovation Reserve and the quarterly review structure—they make evolution automatic so you don’t have to rely on willpower.  
- Low scores on 5 and 6 are normal; this chapter exists to make mentoring and legacy concrete, not abstract aspirations.  
- If you scored low on statement 7, it may be a sign you’re still in “rebuilding” mode. Once the quarterly rhythms lock in, the energy usually returns—because you’re no longer reacting, you’re steering.
