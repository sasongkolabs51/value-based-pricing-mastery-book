## 1. Pre-Adaptation Mindset Check

Before you lock in new rhythms, surface what might quietly resist them. Tick any that resonate.

- [ ] The urgency of the initial crisis has faded, and I feel the pull to just “get back to work” without these reviews.
- [ ] I’m worried that adding quarterly reviews and a roadmap will feel like administrative overhead I don’t have time for.
- [ ] Part of me thinks, “The system works now; why keep tinkering?”
- [ ] I haven’t set aside dedicated innovation funding—every improvement so far has been squeezed in between client demands.
- [ ] The idea of mentoring someone through the full system feels like a big commitment I’m not sure I can keep.
- [ ] I’ve never written a legacy reflection, and the word “legacy” feels too grand for what I’m doing.

If you ticked any of these, you’re feeling exactly what the perpetual edge is designed to overcome. This workbook doesn’t ask for heroic effort; it asks for small, protected rhythms that compound. The goal isn’t more work—it’s smarter, more automatic adaptation.
