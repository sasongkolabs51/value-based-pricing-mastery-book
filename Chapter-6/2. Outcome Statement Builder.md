## 2. Outcome Statement Builder

The foundation of every outcome package is a single paragraph that replaces the task list with a shared definition of success. Use the three-part structure below.

### Template

**Business Context:** Name the client’s strategic objective and the risk that threatens it.  
*“The company intends to [objective], which faces the risk of [specific risk] that could result in [quantified impact].”*

**Success Criterion:** Describe the verifiable condition that tells both sides the engagement has delivered.  
*“Success means [specific, measurable result] that is confirmed by [verification method].”*

**Value Protected or Enabled:** Quantify, even as a range, what this outcome means in business terms.  
*“This outcome protects an estimated $____ in [revenue / market position / avoided penalty] and enables [next step] without [specific obstacle].”*

### Example (Rebecca’s Transfer Pricing Engagement)

- **Business Context:** The company is entering three new jurisdictions with combined first-year revenue projections of $200M. A documentation failure in any jurisdiction could trigger penalty assessments exceeding $120M and delay market access by up to 12 months.
- **Success Criterion:** Delivery of documentation packages for jurisdictions A, B, and C that, after review by the company’s external auditors and local counsel, are confirmed to meet OECD and local standards with zero material deficiencies.
- **Value Protected:** Removes $120M of penalty exposure and ensures uninterrupted revenue realization from all three markets.
