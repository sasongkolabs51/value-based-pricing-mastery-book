## 8. Procurement Pushback Script Builder  
*Purpose: Prepare for the most common objections when you present an engagement letter without hourly reference points.*

---

**Objection 1: “Where’s the hourly breakdown? Our policy requires it for vendor comparison.”**

- **Don’t say:** “We can provide an estimate.”  
- **Redirect:** Politely decline and reframe the comparison basis.  
- *Your script:* “I understand that’s your standard process. The reason we don’t include an hourly breakdown is that this engagement isn’t priced by the hour. We’re quoting a fixed price for a guaranteed outcome. Comparing this to an hourly rate would be like comparing a fixed‑price home renovation to a contractor who bills by the day—they’re fundamentally different. I’m happy to walk you through the value drivers and the risk we’re absorbing so you can compare the total cost against the financial exposure we’re protecting.”

**Objection 2: “What happens if the work takes less time because of AI? Shouldn’t we share the efficiency gain?”**

- **Don’t say:** “We’ll lower the fee.”  
- **Redirect:** Position speed as a client benefit that doesn’t diminish the risk.  
- *Your script:* “The speed is a benefit to you—you get the certainty and the outcome sooner, which means you can deploy capital or launch earlier. The fee reflects the risk we’re taking on and the outcome we guarantee, not the minutes it takes. If our AI tools allow us to deliver that same protection faster, it’s an efficiency that accrues to both of us—you get faster certainty, and we maintain our commitment. Lowering the fee for speed would be like paying less for a faster, more experienced surgeon.”

**Objection 3: “We need the ability to track progress—hourly reporting is how we do that.”**

- **Don’t say:** “We’ll give you regular time summaries.”  
- **Redirect:** Offer outcome‑based progress milestones instead.  
- *Your script:* “Absolutely, transparency is critical. Instead of hours, I’ll provide you with a weekly milestone tracker tied to the outcomes we’ve defined. You’ll see exactly where we are against the success criterion, what’s been completed, and what remains. This is actually a clearer picture of progress than hours, because hours don’t tell you whether you’re closer to the goal; milestones do.”
