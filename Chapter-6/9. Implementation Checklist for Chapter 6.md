## 9. Implementation Checklist for Chapter 6  
*Step‑by‑step to turn your VRR fees into signed outcome‑based engagement letters.*

- [ ] **Re‑read the chapter’s opening story about Rebecca.** Identify the specific clause in her letter that allowed the return to hourly billing. Promise yourself you won’t repeat it.  
- [ ] **Complete the Packaging Readiness Pre‑Check.** Note areas for focused attention.  
- [ ] **Select two engagements** (from your green‑zone or promising yellow‑zone list) for packaging.  
- [ ] **For each engagement, use the Outcome Statement Builder** to draft a one‑paragraph outcome statement. Test it for clarity.  
- [ ] **Classify both engagements using the Archetype Selection Matrix.** Document your reasoning.  
- [ ] **Choose and customize a Value Assurance Mechanism** for each (use the templates in Section 4).  
- [ ] **Draft the Risk Allocation Language** for each engagement, using the appropriate template.  
- [ ] **Assemble a full engagement letter** for each using the Blueprint in Section 6.  
- [ ] **Run the Hourly‑Language Purge Checklist** on your firm’s standard template and on your final drafts. Remove or supersede every offending clause.  
- [ ] **Prepare your pushback scripts** for the three common objections. Rehearse aloud.  
- [ ] **Pilot review:** If possible, have a trusted colleague review the engagement letter as if they were the client. Ask: “Does anything feel like a traditional hourly letter? What questions would you have?”  
- [ ] **Set a date** to present the first letter to the client. You’ll use the retraining conversation tools from Chapter 8 to deliver it effectively.
