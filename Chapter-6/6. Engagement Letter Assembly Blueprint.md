## 6. Engagement Letter Assembly Blueprint

Use this step-by-step guide to build the entire document. The most important rule: the first page must make the hourly rate question irrelevant.

### Page 1: Outcome and Fee Summary

Place these elements on page 1, in order:

1. **Outcome Statement** (from Section 2)  
2. **Fee Summary:** The total fixed fee (and any success fee structure, clearly defined). Add: *“This fee is not based on time spent, but on the value of the outcome described above and the risk the firm assumes in delivering it.”*  
3. **Value Assurance Mechanism** (from Section 4)  
4. **Key Dates / Timeline:** When the outcome will be delivered.

### Page 2+: The Rest (Never the Lead)

- **Scope:** Do not list tasks. Instead, tie scope to the outcome statement. For example: *“The work under this engagement includes all activities necessary to produce the documentation packages meeting the Success Criterion described on page 1.”* If you must list deliverables, describe them as components of the outcome, not hours of work.
- **Risk Allocation** (from Section 5)
- **Client Responsibilities:** What the client must provide (data, access, timely decisions). Link these to the outcome: *“The firm’s ability to meet the Success Criterion depends on the client providing [X] by [date].”*
- **Change Control:** If the outcome definition or client circumstances change materially, fee and timeline are renegotiated in good faith.
- **Standard Terms:** Liability caps, governing law, confidentiality, etc. Keep them. But strip any clause that allows the client to demand hourly backup or ties the fee to time records. If your firm template has an “hourly rate fallback” clause, remove it. If the client’s procurement demands one, use the negotiation script in Section 8.

### Red Flag Removal Checklist

Read your draft engagement letter and answer yes/no:

- [ ] Is there any mention of “hourly rates,” “time spent,” “billable time,” or “timesheets” anywhere? If yes, remove it.
- [ ] Does the scope section read like a list of tasks? Rewrite it as outcome components.
- [ ] Is there a clause allowing the client to demand an hourly breakdown? Remove or replace with alternative dispute resolution focused on outcome delivery.
- [ ] Does the fee section mention “estimate based on anticipated hours”? Rewrite.
- [ ] Does the first page immediately tell the client what success looks like and what it costs? It must.
