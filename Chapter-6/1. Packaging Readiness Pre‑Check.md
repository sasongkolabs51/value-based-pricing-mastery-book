## 1. Packaging Readiness Pre‑Check  
*Before you begin drafting, take an honest look at where your engagement letters currently stand. Rate each statement from 1 (Not at all) to 5 (Completely).*

| Statement | 1–5 |
|-----------|-----|
| My current engagement letters start with a clear statement of the client’s business outcome, not a task list. |  |
| I can point to at least one engagement where the client questioned the invoice because the scope was written as activities rather than results. |  |
| The concept of a “success criterion” that a third party could verify is something I already use informally. |  |
| I have a standard value assurance mechanism (like a guarantee, risk‑sharing, or fixed‑fee with scope‑change provisions) built into my template. |  |
| My engagement letters currently contain clauses that allow a client to demand an hourly breakdown, time records, or rate cards. |  |
| I feel confident that I could explain to a procurement officer why our fee structure doesn’t reference hours—without sounding defensive. |  |
| I have reviewed my firm’s master engagement template and know exactly which clauses need to change for outcome‑based work. |  |

**Interpreting your responses:**  
- If you scored low on statements 1, 3, and 4, the Outcome Statement Builder and the Value Assurance Guide will be your most important tools today.  
- If you scored high on statement 5, your template likely contains a trapdoor back to hourly billing—the Hourly‑Language Purge Checklist addresses that directly.  
- Low confidence on statement 6 is normal; the Pushback Script Builder later in this toolkit will give you the language you need.
