## 3. Archetype Selector

Not every outcome is best packaged the same way. Choose the commercial archetype that fits the service and the client’s buying psychology.

### The Three Archetypes at a Glance

| Archetype | Best for | Value Emphasis | Typical Fee Structure | Language Tone |
|-----------|----------|----------------|-----------------------|---------------|
| **Assurance** | Audit opinions, regulatory certifications, compliance attestations, control assessments | Risk absorption, liability transfer, avoiding harm | Fixed price (VRR heavy on RA) | Standards, independence, “we stand behind this” |
| **Advisory** | Strategy, M&A target evaluation, board counsel, market entry planning | Judgment quality, decision enablement, cost of being wrong | Fixed + optional success bonus (VRR heavy on JI) | Expertise, insight, “this decision is too expensive to get wrong” |
| **Assurance-Plus-Advisory** | Compliance deliverable + board-level interpretation, regulatory filing + strategic recommendation | Dual: risk absorption and strategic judgment | Fixed price, possibly with success element | Balanced: “we deliver the certification and we tell you what it means” |

### Archetype Decision Matrix

Score your engagement on these dimensions, then see which archetype naturally fits.

| Dimension | Score 1–5 (1 = not at all, 5 = absolutely) |
|-----------|:--:|
| The client is primarily buying protection against a defined compliance/regulatory harm. | |
| The client is primarily buying our judgment to make a better strategic decision. | |
| A formal certification, opinion, or signature that carries liability is central to the value. | |
| The value is mostly in the synthesis and interpretation of complex information, not a binary pass/fail outcome. | |
| The client would accept a performance-contingent component. | |

- If your top scores are in rows 1 and 3 → **Assurance**  
- If your top scores are in rows 2 and 4 → **Advisory**  
- If you score high on both sides → **Assurance-Plus-Advisory**
