## 7. Hourly‑Language Purge Checklist  
*Use this checklist to clean your existing engagement letter templates before you adapt them for outcome‑based work. Check each item as you confirm it’s removed or rewritten.*

- [ ] Any clause that allows the client to request an “hourly breakdown” or “time records.”  
- [ ] Any mention of “hourly rates,” “rate cards,” or “standard rates.”  
- [ ] Language that ties the fee to “time and materials” or “hours incurred.”  
- [ ] Language that allows the fee to vary based on “actual time spent” or “efficiency.”  
- [ ] Clauses that define the scope as a “list of tasks” or “activities to be performed.” (Replace with outcome language.)  
- [ ] Clauses that require “daily time reports” or “timesheets” as a deliverable.  
- [ ] Language that implies the client has a right to audit hours or reduce the fee if hours are lower than expected.  
- [ ] Any reference to a “blended rate,” “effective rate,” or other hourly‑derived metric.  
- [ ] Clauses that define “additional services” by reference to an hourly rate list (replace with fixed‑price supplements).  
- [ ] Any mention of “utilization” or internal firm metrics that could inadvertently signal hour‑tracking.

**If you find clauses you cannot remove** (e.g., required by firm policy), add a side letter that supersedes them for this engagement and explicitly states that the fee is outcome‑based and not subject to hourly reconciliation.
