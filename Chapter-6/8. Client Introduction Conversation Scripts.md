## 8. Client Introduction Conversation Scripts

The best engagement letter still needs a human conversation to land properly. Use these scripts as your starting points.

### Script 1: Introducing the New Format to an Existing Client

*“I want to show you something different for our next piece of work. Instead of the usual task list and hourly estimate, this letter defines exactly what success looks like for your business—in this case, [outcome]. The fee is fixed to that outcome, so there are no surprises and no timesheets to review. It’s designed to align our interests completely: we succeed when you get this result, not when we log hours. Take a look at page one—it’s all there.”*

### Script 2: When the Client Asks, “But How Many Hours Is This Based On?”

*“It’s not based on hours at all. It’s based on the value of removing [risk X] and the certainty of getting [outcome Y]. If we deliver that outcome in less time because we’re using the best tools and our most experienced people, that’s a win for you—you get the result faster, at a price you already know. The fee reflects what the outcome is worth, not how long it takes.”*

### Script 3: When Procurement Demands an Hourly Backup Clause

*“I understand you need assurance on value. We’re happy to build in an alternative: if you believe the outcome hasn’t been met as defined on page one, we’ll address it directly through a review process focused on the success criteria—not hours. The outcome is what matters; let’s keep the conversation there. If your policy absolutely requires a time-based fallback, we can discuss it, but I’d rather you judge us on results.”*

**Practice:** Choose the script most relevant to your upcoming conversation and rehearse it aloud three times.
